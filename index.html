Cybersecurity Awareness
🔒
Always check HTTPS
Legitimate sites use HTTPS. If you see HTTP (no 'S'), your data is unencrypted and vulnerable to interception. Look for the padlock icon in your browser.
🔍
Verify the domain carefully
Attackers use lookalike domains like "paypa1.com" or "arnazon.com". Always double-check the spelling of the domain before entering credentials.
⚡
Beware of urgency tactics
Phishing sites create false urgency — "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!" Legitimate organizations don't pressure you to act immediately.
🔗
Avoid URL shorteners
Short URLs hide the real destination. Use tools like this scanner to expand and check shortened links before clicking them, especially in emails or messages.
🎣
Spot phishing keywords
URLs with words like "login", "verify", "secure", "update", "free", "prize", or "bank" in unusual domains are red flags. Legitimate services use their own domains.
📧
Email link safety
Never click links directly in suspicious emails. Instead, navigate to the official website manually or use a bookmark you created yourself previously.
